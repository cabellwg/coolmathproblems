cool math problems

To suggest other cool problems, email coolmathproblems@gmail.com or submit a pull request. Problems are organized by prerequisite knowledge, not difficulty. Some problems are trivial; others are quite difficult.

elementary

To organize a secret Santa exchange, $n$ people each put their own name into a hat. Then, they pass the hat around in a circle, each person choosing a name at random. As $n$ increases, what does the probability that no person will draw their own name approach?

Show that the average of any two consecutive odd prime numbers is composite.

Assume that no one has more than 200,000 hairs on their head. Assume also that London has a population of 8.7 million. Prove that there are two people in London with the exact same number of hairs on their head.

What is the expected value of the minimum of $n$ numbers randomly chosen from the unit interval $(0, 1)$?

undergraduate

Do there exist groups $G$ and $H$ distinct up to isomorphism such that $G \le H \le G$, where $\le$ is the subgroup relation?

Find three disjoint open sets in the standard topology on $\mathbb{R}$ which share the same (non-empty!) boundary set.
